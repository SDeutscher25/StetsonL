Chocolate Chip Skillet Cookie (Pizookie Recipe)

Ingredients 1x Amount 2x Amount 3x Amount
Softened Salted Butter 1/2 Cup 1 Cup 1 1/2 Cups
Light Brown Sugar 1/2 Cup 1 Cup 1 1/2 Cups
Granulated Sugar 1/2 Cup 1 Cup 1 1/2 Cups
Egg (room temp) 1 2 3
Pure Vanilla Extract 1 Teaspoons 2 Teaspoons 3 Teaspoons
Whole Milk (room temp) 2 Tablespoons 4 Tablespoons 6 Tablespoons
All-Purpose Flour 1 1/4 Cups 2 1/2 Cups 3.8 Cups
Fine Sea Salt 1/4 Teaspoon 1/2 Teaspoon 0.8 Teaspoon
Baking Soda 1/2 Teaspoon 1 Teaspoon 1 1/2 Teaspoons
Baking Powder 1/4 Teaspoon 1/2 Teaspoon 0.8 Teaspoon
Chocolate Chips + extra for the top 1 Cup 2 Cups 3 Cups

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.
  2. Lightly grease a 10" cast iron skillet. Set aside.
  3. Cream together butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar in the bowl of a standing mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, or in a large mixing bowl with a hand-held mixer.
  4. Add egg, milk, and vanilla, and beat until combined.
  5. Add flour, sea salt, baking soda, and baking powder. Beat, starting on low speed and increasing to high as the dry ingredients get incorporated. Beat until a slightly stick dough form.
  6. Add chocolate chips and stir in by hand.
  7. Pour batter into the prepared cast iron skillet.
  8. Use a greased spatula to evenly spread out the cookie dough in the skillet.
  9. Sprinkle a handful of extra chocolate chips on top (if desired).
  10. Bake for 15 minutes, or until the sides are just browning/set and the middle is still slightly jiggly. Please do not over-bake. Remember the skillet cookie will continue to bake after it is removed from the oven.
  11. Let cool for 5-10 minutes.
  12. Serve warm with ice cream and a drizzle of chocolate sauce!

Apple Pie

Apple Pie Filling

Ingredients 1x Amount 2x Amount 3x Amount
Apples peeled, cored, and sliced 10 Cups 20 Cups 30 Cups
Granulated Sugar 1/2 Cup 1 Cup 1 1/2 Cups
All-Purpose Flour 3 Tablespoons 6 Tablespoons 9 Tablespoons
Ground Cinnamon 2 Teaspoons 4 Teaspoons 6 Teaspoons

+ Optional Dash of Nutmeg

Double pie Crust

All-Purpose Flour 2 1/2 Cups 5 Cups 7 1/2 Cups
Sea Salt 1 Teaspoon 2 Teaspoons 3 Teaspoons
Granulated Sugar 2 Tablespoons 4 Tablespoons 6 Tablespoons
Butter COLD, cut into 1 inch pieces 1 Cup 2 Cups 3 Cups
Ice Cold Water 1/2 Cup 1 Cup 1 1/2 Cup

Egg Wash (optional)

Egg, beaten 1 2 3
Milk 1 Tablespoon 2 Tablespoons 3 Tablespoons

Instructions

Make the Crust

  1. Place all pie crust ingredients in the order listed into a food processor fitted with an “S” blade process for 15-40 seconds, or until the mixture resembles a coarse meal and holds together when pinched.
  2. Remove dough from processor (it will be crumbly), transfer it to a large bowl or flat surface, and form into two, equal-sized round discs.
  3. Wrap both discs of dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight.

Make the Filling

  1. Place the peeled and cored apples in a microwave safe dish and cook for 6 minutes, until apples are slightly soft.
  2. While apples are in the microwave, in a small bowl, mix together sugar, flour cinnamon and nutmeg.
  3. Once apples are finished cooking in the microwave, put them in a colander to drain excess water. Let them cool in the colander for at least 10 minutes.
  4. Transfer cooled and drained apples to a bowl, add dry ingredient mixture and stir until it’s evenly distributed.

Assemble

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Remove crusts from refrigerator and roll into circles, about ¼ inch thick. (you may need to let them sit on the counter at room temperature for 5 minutes, or until it rolls a little easier).
  3. Gently place on round crust into pie plate.
  4. Pour filling into unbaked pie crust.
  5. Place second crust on top of the apple pie filling and shape the edges. Cut 4 slits in the top to vent steam (or make a lattice crust).

Bake and Cool

  1. Place a pie crust shield or aluminum foil over the outer crust of the pie to protect it from burning. Remove shield in the last 10-15 minutes of baking if it doesn’t begin to brown under the shield.
  2. Bake the pie on the middle/lower rack in the preheated oven for 60 minutes or until the filling begins to bubble and the top is lightly browned.
  3. Let cool for at least 3 hours (the filling thickens as it cools).
  4. Serve with vanilla ice cream.

Coffee Cookies

Ingredients 1x Amount 2x Amount 3x Amount
Softened Salted Butter 1/2 Cup 1 Cup 1 1/2 Cups
Granulated Sugar 1/2 Cup 1 Cup 1 1/2 Cups
Packed Light Brown Sugar 1/2 Cup 1 Cup 1 1/2 Cups
Pure Vanilla Extract 1 Teaspoons 2 Teaspoons 3 Teaspoons
Egg (room temp) 1 2 3
All-Purpose Flour 1 1/4 Cups 2 1/2 Cups 3.8 Cups
Unsweetened Cocoa Powder 1/4 Cup 1/2 Cup 0.8 Cup
Epresso Powder 1 Tablespoon 2 Tablespoons 3 Tablespoons
Baking Soda 1/2 Teaspoon 1 Teaspoon 1 1/2 Teaspoons
Salt 1/2 Teaspoon 1 Teaspoon 1 1/2 Teaspoons
Baking Powder 1/4 Teaspoon 1/2 Teaspoon 0.8 Teaspoon
Chocolate Chips 1 Cup 2 Cups 3 Cups

Instructions

  1. In a medium bowl combine flour, cocoa powder, espresso powder, baking soda, sea salt, baking powder. Set aside.
  2. In the bowl of a standing mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, or in a large bowl with a hand mixer, cream together butter, brown sugar and granulated sugar until combined.
  3. Beat in egg and vanilla until fluffy.
  4. Mix in the dry ingredients until combined.
  5. Add chocolate chips and beat on low speed to combine.
  6. Transfer the dough to an airtight container and chill for at least 30 minutes.
  7. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper and set aside.
  8. Use a 1 ½ to 2 Tablespoon cookie scoop to measure out dough and then roll it into balls. Place on prepared cookie sheets evenly spaced, with at least 1 ½” between the cookies.
  9. Bake in preheated oven for approximately 8-9 minutes. Take them out when they are just barely set, and starting to crackle around the edges.
  10. Let them sit on the baking pan for 5 minutes before removing to cooling rack to cool completely.
